Staples and Most Iconic SF Characters

Ryu

Catchphrase: “The answer lies in the heart of battle.”

Short bio / origin

Wandering Japanese martial artist seeking to improve his skills and find true strength.

Debut: 1987, Street Fighter.

Fun fact / rivalry

Famous rivalry with Ken; inspiration for countless fighting‑game protagonists.

Signature move(s)

Ken

Catchphrase: "Come on!".

Short bio / origin

Ryu’s best friend and American rival, trained under the same master, Gouken.

Debut: 1987, Street Fighter.

Fun fact / rivalry

Friendly but intense rivalry with Ryu; married to Eliza.

Signature move(s)

Chun-Li

Catchphrase: "Yatta!"

Short bio / origin

Interpol detective from China, seeking justice and revenge for her father’s disappearance.

Debut: 1991, Street Fighter II.

Fun fact / rivalry

First female fighter in the series and the genre; frequent rival of M. Bison.

Signature move(s)

Vega

Catchphrase: "Victory is beautiful" and "Beauty is Power!"

Short bio / origin

Spanish ninja and assassin obsessed with beauty.

Debut: 1991, Street Fighter II.

Fun fact / rivalry

Uses a claw and mask, moves quickly on the wall.

Signature move(s)

Dhalsim

Catchphrase:  "I will meditate and then destroy you”

Short bio / origin

Indian yoga master and pacifist fighting for his village's well-being.

Debut: 1991, Street Fighter II.

Fun fact / rivalry

Often depicted as meditative, fights only for a noble cause.

Signature move(s)

Dudley

Catchphrase: “Gutter trash!”.

Short bio / origin

Classy English heavyweight boxer on a quest to retrieve his father’s car.

Debut: 1999, Street Fighter III: 2nd Impact.

Fun fact / rivalry

Distinct for never using kicks, entirely boxing-based moveset; rival to Balrog.

Signature move(s)

Blanka

Catchphrase: “Rahhhh!”

Short bio / origin

Wild man from Brazil, mutated by an airplane crash and raised in the jungle.

Debut: 1991, Street Fighter II.

Fun fact / rivalry

Real name Jimmy; known for biting and electricity attacks.

Signature move(s)

M.Bison

Catchphrase: "Now face the mighty Bison!"

Short bio / origin

Evil mastermind and leader of Shadaloo criminal organization.

Debut: 1991, Street Fighter II (final boss).

Fun fact / rivalry

Changes bodies to prolong his life; main series villain.

Signature move(s)

E.Honda

Catchphrase: "You hungry? Here's some beef!"

Short bio / origin

Sumo wrestler striving to prove sumo’s worth internationally.

Debut: 1991, Street Fighter II.

Fun fact / rivalry

Baths often feature in his endings; friendly rivalry with Zangief.

Signature move(s)

Balrog

Catchphrase: "I’ll make you pay!”

Short bio / origin

Ruthless Las Vegas boxer expelled from pro fighting for violence.

Debut: 1991, Street Fighter II.

Fun fact / rivalry

Inspired by Mike Tyson, loves money over honor.

Signature move(s)

Guile

Catchphrase: "Go home and be a family man!"

Short bio / origin

American Air Force major on a mission to avenge his friend Charlie Nash’s death.

Debut: 1991, Street Fighter II.

Fun fact / rivalry

Known for his unique flat-top haircut and U.S. patriotism.

Signature move(s)

Ibuki

Catchphrase: "Ibuki on the scene!".

Short bio / origin

Teenage ninja girl balancing life as a fighter and a student in a sleepy Japanese village.

Debut: 1997, Street Fighter III: New Generation.

Fun fact / rivalry

Known for her speed and tricky ninja moves; dislikes having to fight instead of hang out with friends.

Signature move(s)

Sakura

Catchphrase: "Let's go!".

Short bio / origin

Energetic Japanese schoolgirl who idolizes Ryu and imitates his style.

Debut: 1996, Street Fighter Alpha 2.

Fun fact / rivalry

Is a self-taught fighter; often used for comic relief and youthful energy.

Signature move(s)

Dan

Catchphrase: "Saikyo-ryu!"

Short bio / origin

Goofy, pink-gi martial artist parodying other fighting game heroes; seeks to avenge his father.

Debut: 1996, Street Fighter Alpha.

Fun fact / rivalry

Known for his intentionally weak and comedic fighting style; joke rival to Sagat.

Signature move(s)

Akuma

Catchphrase: "I am Akuma, and I will teach you the true meaning of pain!"

Short bio / origin

Ryu’s and Ken’s “uncle,” a brutal master of the dark arts, seeks the ultimate fight.

Debut: 1994, Super Street Fighter II Turbo (secret boss).

Fun fact / rivalry

Known for epic boss battles; is a hidden/secret character in many games.

Signature move(s)

Zangief

Catchphrase: "I am the strongest in the world!"

Short bio / origin

Russian pro wrestler, proud of his strength and homeland.

Debut: 1991, Street Fighter II.

Fun fact / rivalry

"Red Cyclone" who trains with bears in Siberia; friendly rivalry with Ryu and Honda.

Signature move(s)

Sagat

Catchphrase: "Tiger!"

Short bio / origin

Muay Thai master and former champion, initially a villain and Ryu’s main rival.

Debut: 1987, Street Fighter (final boss).

Fun fact / rivalry

Famous chest scar from Ryu’s “Shoryuken”.

Signature move(s)

Juri

Catchphrase: “I'm going to enjoy this!”.

Short bio / origin

Sadistic Taekwondo fighter enhanced with the “Feng Shui Engine” eye, loves chaos.

Debut: 2010, Super Street Fighter IV.

Fun fact / rivalry

First Korean character; popular for her villainous charm and unique playstyle.
